US and China agree framework of trade deal ahead of Trump-Xi meeting
BBC World News • 27 Oct 2025, 5:00 AM NZDT • Priority: 9/10
The US and China have reportedly agreed on a framework for a potential trade deal in advance of a meeting between President Trump and President Xi Jinping. US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent indicated this framework includes resolutions on TikTok, rare earth mineral controls, a potential tariff avoidance, and renewed soybean purchases.
Why this matters: This signals a potential de-escalation of the trade war between the world's two largest economies, which has been causing significant uncertainty and impacting businesses globally. A thawing of relations could lead to more predictable trade flows and potentially boost economic growth for both nations, as well as the wider international community. This agreement may also point towards a pragmatic approach to US-China relations, at least on the economic front, as both countries seek to avoid further disruptions.

American Airlines is arriving late to the luxury travel boom. Can it catch up?
CNBC Finance • 27 Oct 2025, 1:03 AM NZDT
American Airlines is belatedly trying to capitalize on the surge in luxury travel, trailing far behind Delta and United who are already reaping significant profits from this booming sector. Despite offering the most flights, American only captured a minuscule portion of the profit earned by the top three US carriers from luxury travel this year.
Why this matters: This highlights a significant competitive disadvantage for American Airlines in a lucrative and growing market segment. The failure to capture a substantial share of the luxury travel boom could signal a need for American to revamp its offerings and strategies to better attract high-spending customers, or risk further financial losses compared to its rivals. The success of Delta and United also indicates that there is a considerable appetite and profitability within the current market for luxury travel options, particularly post-pandemic.
