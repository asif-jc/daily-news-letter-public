Ultimately, it underscores the importance of prioritizing the well-being of essential workers for the smooth operation of the entire transportation system.

Brazil's Amazon rainforest at risk as key protection under threat
BBC World News • 17 Nov 2025, 2:10 PM NZDT • Priority: 6/10
Brazil's Amazon rainforest is facing a renewed threat of deforestation as powerful farming interests and some politicians are trying to overturn a ban on selling soya grown on land cleared after 2008. Environmental groups are warning that removing this ban, credited with curbing deforestation, would be disastrous and lead to increased land grabbing for soya production.
Why this matters: This potential policy shift has significant implications for global supply chains, particularly in sectors like meat production that rely on Brazilian soya for animal feed. If the ban is lifted and deforestation accelerates, it could push the Amazon closer to a critical tipping point, further disrupting ecosystems, biodiversity, and global climate patterns, and potentially impacting businesses that depend on stable environmental conditions and consumer trust.
