Flights returning to normal after Airbus warning grounded planes
BBC World News • 30 Nov 2025, 1:37 AM NZDT • Priority: 7/10
Thousands of Airbus A320 planes were temporarily grounded after Airbus issued a warning about potential interference from solar radiation impacting onboard computers, causing flight disruptions. The aerospace giant, along with airlines like Air France and American Airlines, worked to implement software updates or computer replacements to address the issue.
Why this matters: This incident highlights the increasing vulnerability of modern aviation to external factors like solar activity and emphasizes the importance of robust software and hardware to mitigate these risks. The quick response and relatively smooth update process signal a growing awareness and preparedness within the industry, but also underscores the potential for significant operational disruptions caused by unforeseen technological vulnerabilities.

Achieving lasting remission for HIV
Ars Technica • 30 Nov 2025, 1:15 AM NZDT • Priority: 7/10
An article in Knowable Magazine from November 2025 highlights promising trials using engineered antibodies that suggest a "functional cure" for HIV might be within reach. This offers hope for the approximately 40 million people worldwide living with the virus.
Why this matters: A functional cure for HIV, even if not a complete eradication, would dramatically improve the quality of life for millions, potentially reducing the need for lifelong antiretroviral therapy. This research signals significant progress in immunology and gene therapy, indicating a potential shift toward managing chronic viral infections with innovative, long-term solutions.

How Black Friday became a retail letdown: 'To sustain the ride, they started to dilute it'
CNBC Finance • 29 Nov 2025, 6:29 AM NZDT
Black Friday's traditional image of massive crowds and unbeatable deals is fading, as retailers dilute the event with extended sales periods and consumers become skeptical of true savings. Experts like Mark Cohen, former CEO of Sears Canada, argue that the "integrity" of Black Friday as a unique promotional event has been compromised.
Why this matters: The article highlights a crucial shift in consumer behavior and retail strategy, suggesting that traditional "event" shopping is losing its appeal. This forces businesses to rethink their promotional calendars and potentially prioritize online strategies and consistent value offerings over a single, highly-hyped day. Understanding this evolution is critical for retailers aiming to stay competitive in an increasingly digital and price-conscious market.
