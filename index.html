Quantum computers need vastly fewer resources than thought to break vital encryption
Ars Technica • 01 Apr 2026, 7:25 AM NZDT • Priority: 9/10
Two independent research papers have shown that building a quantum computer capable of breaking elliptic-curve cryptography (ECC), a cornerstone of modern encryption, requires significantly fewer resources than previously thought, with one group achieving it in 10 days and another in under 9 minutes. Researchers from diverse backgrounds are developing novel quantum architectures and improving algorithms to accelerate progress toward cryptographically relevant quantum computing (CRQC).
Why this matters: This is a big deal because ECC secures everything from banking transactions to cryptocurrency blockchains, and a quantum computer capable of cracking it would have enormous implications for security. These advances signal that the threat of quantum computers rendering current encryption methods obsolete is accelerating, requiring businesses and governments to invest now in quantum-resistant cryptography to protect sensitive data in the long run.
