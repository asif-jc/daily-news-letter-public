Police seize €1.3bn from Campari owner over alleged tax evasion
BBC World News • 02 Nov 2025, 12:27 AM NZDT • Priority: 8/10
Italian police have seized €1.5 billion worth of shares from Lagfin, the Luxembourg-based company controlling Campari Group, alleging tax evasion related to the absorption of its Italian arm. The investigation centers on the potential failure to pay taxes equivalent to the value of the seized shares during the merger, and Campari chairman Luca Garavoglia is reportedly under investigation.
Why this matters: This case highlights the increasing scrutiny of multinational corporations and their tax practices, particularly regarding cross-border mergers and acquisitions. The significant value involved and the high-profile nature of Campari suggest a potential trend toward more aggressive tax enforcement by European authorities, potentially impacting other large companies operating internationally and emphasizing the importance of rigorous tax compliance.
