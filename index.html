BBC World News • 28 Sep 2025, 11:49 PM NZDT • Priority: 7/10
UN economic and military sanctions have been reimposed on Iran after the UK, France, and Germany activated the "snapback" mechanism, citing Iran's continued nuclear escalation and lack of cooperation. This action comes 10 years after the original deal that lifted sanctions in exchange for limitations on Iran's nuclear program, a deal that began to unravel when the US withdrew in 2018.
Why this matters: This development signifies a major setback for international diplomacy and heightens geopolitical instability in the Middle East. Businesses should be concerned as these sanctions could disrupt regional trade, increase energy prices, and potentially lead to further conflict, requiring them to reassess their risk exposure and supply chain dependencies.
