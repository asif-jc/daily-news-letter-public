Microsoft aims to move up to 80% of Surface and data center component manufacturing outside of China by 2026, while Amazon and Google are exploring alternative suppliers and manufacturing locations in Southeast Asia.
Why this matters: This shift signifies a significant decoupling trend in the tech industry, reflecting growing concerns about political risks and supply chain vulnerabilities associated with China. Business professionals should pay attention because this trend will likely impact global supply chain strategies, potentially increasing costs and requiring companies to diversify their manufacturing and sourcing operations. The move also foreshadows potential investment opportunities in alternative manufacturing hubs in Southeast Asia and elsewhere.
