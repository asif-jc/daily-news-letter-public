More than 1,400 flights cancelled as US air traffic cuts enter second day
BBC World News • 09 Nov 2025, 8:55 PM NZDT • Priority: 9/10
Over 1,400 flights were cancelled and thousands more delayed across the US as the FAA implemented air traffic cuts due to air traffic controllers reporting fatigue during the ongoing government shutdown. The shutdown, now the longest in history, stems from a political impasse between Republicans and Democrats in Congress over funding and health care subsidies.
Why this matters: This situation highlights the tangible impact of political gridlock on critical infrastructure and the broader economy. Business travelers and supply chains are disrupted, and the cascading effects could further erode consumer confidence, signaling a need for businesses to prepare for potential operational challenges related to government instability. It also underscores the crucial role of essential workers like air traffic controllers and the risks involved when they are stretched thin.

US authorities ground cargo plane model after Kentucky air disaster
BBC World News • 09 Nov 2025, 4:12 PM NZDT • Priority: 8/10
Following a catastrophic crash of a UPS Boeing MD-11 in Kentucky that resulted in 14 deaths, US authorities have grounded all MD-11 and MD-11F cargo planes, affecting UPS, FedEx, and Western Global Airlines. The FAA took action after an engine fire caused the plane to crash during takeoff.
Why this matters: This grounding is a significant disruption to global cargo operations, potentially impacting delivery times and supply chains for businesses reliant on air freight. The incident will likely trigger increased scrutiny of aging aircraft models and maintenance protocols within the cargo industry, and may signal a move towards prioritizing newer, more fuel-efficient aircraft. This situation may also lead to increased insurance premiums for cargo operators in the future.
