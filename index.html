Wikipedia urges AI companies to use its paid API, and stop scraping
TechCrunch • 11 Nov 2025, 7:30 AM NZDT • Priority: 7/10
Wikipedia, run by the Wikimedia Foundation, is urging AI developers to switch from scraping its website for data to using its paid API, the Wikimedia Enterprise platform. This move is designed to ensure proper attribution to Wikipedia's contributors and alleviate the burden on its servers caused by AI bots.
Why this matters: This situation highlights the tension between open-source information and the commercial interests of AI companies, signaling a potential shift in how data is accessed and used to train AI models. It also raises questions about the sustainability of free information resources like Wikipedia as AI's hunger for data continues to grow and potentially impacts its human user base and funding. This move could also set a precedent for other content providers to follow in establishing clearer boundaries and monetization strategies regarding the use of their data for AI development.
