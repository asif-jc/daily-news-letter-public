China restricts export of metals vital for making electronics
BBC World News • 10 Oct 2025, 12:08 AM NZDT • Priority: 9/10
China has tightened export controls on rare earth metals, lithium batteries, and certain forms of graphite - materials crucial for electronics and advanced tech - requiring government approval for exports even in small amounts. This move, impacting companies globally, especially those in the US, is seen as a bargaining tactic amid ongoing trade negotiations and an anticipated meeting between Chinese leader Xi Jinping and US President Donald Trump.
Why this matters: This action signals a potential escalation in the trade tensions between China and the US, and highlights China's dominance in the production of critical materials. It underscores the vulnerability of global supply chains that rely heavily on Chinese exports, potentially forcing companies to diversify their sourcing, invest in alternative materials, or relocate production, all of which could increase costs and disrupt markets. Businesses should start assessing their reliance on these materials and consider strategies for mitigating the risk of further supply disruptions.
