BBC World News • 03 Nov 2025, 6:45 PM NZDT • Priority: 7/10

Why this matters: This incident highlights the increasing pressure from authoritarian regimes on academic institutions worldwide, forcing them to choose between principles and financial gain. It signals a chilling effect on human rights research and academic freedom, raising concerns about the integrity of scholarship and the potential for self-censorship within universities seeking to maintain access to lucrative international student markets. This situation also underscores the complex balancing act businesses and institutions face when operating in countries with differing values.

Maldives bans smoking for younger generations
BBC World News • 04 Nov 2025, 1:55 AM NZDT • Priority: 6/10
The Maldives has instituted a nationwide ban on tobacco for anyone born on or after January 1, 2007, making it the only country to enforce such a generational prohibition. This initiative, championed by the Maldivian health ministry and the archipelago's tobacco control board, aims to protect young people from the harms of tobacco use.
Why this matters: This ban signals a potential shift in global approaches to tobacco control, highlighting a move towards more radical, preventative measures that target future generations. It could prompt other nations to consider similar legislation, particularly those already grappling with high rates of youth smoking or vaping, and raises questions about the long-term efficacy and ethical considerations of such policies.
