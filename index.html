FCC to rescind ruling that said ISPs are required to secure their networks
Ars Technica • 01 Nov 2025, 9:44 AM NZDT • Priority: 9/10
The FCC is planning to repeal a January 2025 ruling that required ISPs to secure their networks, citing concerns about the agency's authority and effectiveness. FCC Chairman Brendan Carr is advocating for relying on ISPs' voluntary commitments instead, arguing that carriers are already taking steps to improve cybersecurity.
Why this matters: This shift could significantly weaken cybersecurity protections for internet users, as voluntary commitments are often less enforceable than regulatory mandates. It signals a potential trend towards deregulation within the telecommunications industry, potentially leaving networks more vulnerable to cyberattacks from actors like China and impacting data privacy and security for businesses and individuals alike.
