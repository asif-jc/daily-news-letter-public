Trump admin demands states exempt ISPs from net neutrality and price laws
Ars Technica • 31 Oct 2025, 8:26 AM NZDT • Priority: 9/10
The Trump administration is demanding that states receiving federal broadband deployment grants through the $42 billion BEAD program exempt internet service providers (ISPs) from net neutrality and price regulations across the entire state. According to the Commerce Department, net neutrality is considered a form of rate regulation, which is prohibited under the law that created the BEAD program.
Why this matters: This move could significantly impact the future of internet access and regulation, potentially leading to ISPs having more control over pricing and content prioritization. Business professionals should care because this could affect the cost and quality of internet service for their companies and employees, while also stifling innovation and competition if ISPs can favor certain services over others. Ultimately, this signals a continued effort to deregulate the internet and shift control away from consumers and states to private providers.
