BBC World News • 06 Apr 2026, 11:19 AM NZST • Priority: 9/10
Spain's pork industry is facing significant financial losses due to the threat of African Swine Fever (ASF), even though the outbreak is currently contained. Pig farmers like Jordi Saltiveri are seeing the value of their livestock plummet, as the mere presence of ASF in the country scares away importers.
Why this matters: This situation highlights the vulnerability of global agricultural markets to disease outbreaks and the economic ripple effects that can quickly spread far beyond the immediate infected area.
