BBC World News • 08 Oct 2025, 12:37 AM NZDT • Priority: 9/10

While developing nations, especially China, are leading the charge, wealthier nations like the US and EU are increasingly reliant on fossil fuels, a trend that's likely to widen according to the IEA.
Why this matters: This milestone highlights the increasing viability and adoption of renewable energy globally, signalling a potential shift away from traditional fossil fuels, although there is still work to do. However, the diverging paths between developed and developing nations present challenges to a unified global response to climate change and could impact international energy policy and investment strategies.
