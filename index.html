China to ease chip export ban in new trade deal, White House says
BBC World News • 03 Nov 2025, 3:42 AM NZDT • Priority: 9/10
The White House announced that China will ease its export ban on automotive computer chips as part of a newly struck trade deal with the US. This agreement, reached after discussions between Xi Jinping and Donald Trump, aims to de-escalate the ongoing trade war between the two nations.
Why this matters: This signals a potential thaw in the US-China trade war, reducing uncertainty for global businesses, particularly those in the automotive industry who rely on these chips. While the deal includes concessions on both sides, like US soybean exports and rare earth minerals, the fact that chip exports are being addressed underscores the importance of the global semiconductor supply chain and its impact on various sectors.

Rising energy prices put AI and data centers in the crosshairs
TechCrunch • 02 Nov 2025, 5:15 AM NZDT • Priority: 7/10
A recent survey commissioned by Sunrun reveals that 80% of consumers are concerned about the impact of growing data center electricity consumption, fueled by AI, on their utility bills. This concern comes as data centers now consume 4% of US electricity, more than double their share from 2018.
Why this matters: This signals a growing tension between technological advancements like AI and the increasing cost of energy for consumers. If left unaddressed, this could lead to public pushback against further data center expansion and AI development, potentially impacting the growth trajectory of these industries and increasing the pressure for more sustainable energy solutions.
